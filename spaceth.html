SpaceTH
Vanue Renting Platform

UX/UI, Branding
Role: Designer
Client: SpaceTH

SpaceTH was an online marketplace that makes it easier to find and book unique spaces for any activity. The five main categories are commercial, corporate, celebration, production, and lifestyle.

My role in this project was designing brand identity, product UX/UI, and also developing front-end user interface for the MVP.

Beginning with identity design, I came up with the idea “A space is meaningless, without a user making use of it in a meaningful way.” So I designed the logo to represent a blank space and played with how to combine it with different elements to create different meanings. As a result, the primary logo can be extended to make sub-brand logos for each of the five categories. For mood and tone, I aimed to convey a professional, fun, and inviting feeling, with shapes and colors. Additionally, I made the logo variable so it can adapt based on context, to create a multi-platform and scalable branding.

Designing UX/UI in the MVP stage, we focused on two main flows consisting of listing creation and discovery, allowing space owners to add their listing and users to discover spaces.

We did interviews and testing with space owners to understand their insight. There were three biggest pain points: the forms are lengthy, it’s not practical to finish the form at one time, and it's hard to imagine what the finished page will look like during creation. My solution was to create a WYSIWYG editor with the fewest required fields possible, Providing a preview function, a save draft function, and a completeness indicator. This allowed users to draft and come back to finish the listing later before submitting it.

For discovery flow, the challenge was that data vary so much from one category to another, a hotel room has very different properties from a swimming pool for example. The UI solutions I came up with include sub-landing pages, tagging, color coding, and context-based search form and filters, to make it easier for a user to navigate through categories with fewer extra steps.

To ensure consistency throughout the platform and make a unified brand experience, I created a design system with detailed UI guidelines, an icon set, and illustrations.

In MVP development I worked on the front-end coding creating responsive views using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, in collaboration with a back-end developer who developed a database, API, and integration.
